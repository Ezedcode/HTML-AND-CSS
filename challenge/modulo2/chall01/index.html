The history of android mascot

You know that the system operation Android, kept by Google is one of the most used for mobile device in all world. But you don't know that your nice mascot has a curios name and a history? Well follow this article to learn more things about this little robot.

The first version

The first attempt to create a mascot arose at 2007 and comes by a developer called Dan Morrill. he tells that he open the Inkscape(free software for image vectorization) and created your own robot version. the goal was just to personify the system just for your team, no request company to create a mascot.

This first bizarre version was named in tribute of your creator: would be the Dandroids.

A new mascot appears

The idea of having a mascot was maturing and the mission was passed for a professional in the field. The illustrator Irina Block, also Google employees, stayed with the mission to represent the little robot in more pleasant way.

Irene's main idea was to represent all graphically with few traces and in a flatter way. The drawing also should generate quick identification with who looks at he. So appears the Bugdroid the new mascot of Android.

The main inspiration for the traces of the new Bugdroid came from that toy that illustrate bathroom doors for indicate the general of each door. Tells the legend that the artist was creating on your table at the desk's Google and looks for the side of bathroom and the identification was immediate: simple, clear, objective.

Do you wanna learn more?

Another curios subject about the Android is that each version always was named in tribute a candy, in alphabetical order from version 1.5 until 9.0.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 3.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lolipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately,the Android Q not exist, well the Google solved to put the end at this fun practice and started using numbers, what give rise to Android 10.
